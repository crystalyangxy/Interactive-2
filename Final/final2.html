Floating porcelain bowls clink together as they
circulate gently on water, producing a percussive
soundscape of unexpected musicality.

The French artist Céleste Boursier-Mougenot has
brought the architectural feature of the gazing pool
indoors to create what he describes as “a kind of
dream.” He explains, “The bowls are moved by an
invisible force, and the presence of water is fascinating
It makes for a totally natural connection between
the objects. . . . I am spontaneously drawn by this
type of state, between incertitude and floating.”
